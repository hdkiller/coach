This guide walks a new coach from first login through a working roster and calendar. For the full topic map, see the [Coaches](/documentation/coaches) index.

## Before you begin

You need:

- A Coach Watts account with coaching surfaces enabled
- At least one way to reach athletes (email, share link, or your public Start page)
- Optional: a small library of workout or plan templates to schedule in week one

Platform subscription and AI quotas are managed under **Settings → Billing** — see [Account & Billing](/documentation/help/account-billing).

## Week-one path

### 1. Set your public presence

1. Open **Profile → Settings → Public Presence**.
2. Choose a coach slug, bio, and branding you are comfortable sharing.
3. Enable or configure the **Start page** and **direct invite** experience if you want inbound requests.

See [Coach Settings & Public Presence](/documentation/coaches/coach-settings-public-presence) and [Start & Direct Invite Pages](/documentation/coaches/start-direct-invite-pages).

### 2. Connect your first athlete

Open **Coaching → Athletes → Invite Athlete** and pick a path:

| Method             | When to use                                     |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------------------- |
| Share link / QR    | Open cohort or social post                      |
| Invite by email    | One known athlete                               |
| Connect by code    | Athlete already has an account and a coach code |
| Start page request | Prospect fills intake; you approve or decline   |

Details: [Connecting with Athletes](/documentation/coaches/connecting-athletes) and [Invitations & Coaching Requests](/documentation/coaches/invitations-requests).

![Athletes roster and invite tools under Coaching → Athletes](/media/docs/coaching-athletes.webp)

Coach Watts does **not** create a secret login for the athlete on your behalf. Athletes join with their own account through an invite, code, or Start page request.

### 3. Learn the Overview

**Coaching → Overview** shows weekly compliance signals, recent athlete activity, and shortcuts into the roster and libraries. Use it as your morning triage board. See [Strategic Overview](/documentation/coaches/strategic-overview).

![Coaching strategic overview with roster shortcuts and connect-first-athlete empty state](/media/docs/coaching-overview.webp)

### 4. Open an athlete profile

From **Coaching → Athletes**, open an athlete to review:

- Fitness / form / readiness style signals
- Upcoming and recent sessions
- Zones and recommendations the coach role can see

Stay in coach-scoped views — you do not need the athlete's password. See [Athlete Roster & Profiles](/documentation/coaches/athlete-roster-profiles) and [Reviewing an Athlete](/documentation/coaches/acting-as-athlete).

### 5. Schedule on the coach calendar

1. Open **Coaching → Calendar**.
2. Select the athlete (and optional second calendar for comparison).
3. Use the **Workouts** or **Plans** drawer to place templates on dates.
4. Move or copy sessions as the week changes.

See [Coaching Calendar](/documentation/coaches/coaching-calendar).

![Coach calendar for reviewing athlete schedules and placing library workouts](/media/docs/coaching-calendar.webp)

### 6. Seed your library

Build reusable content under **Library**:

- **Workouts** — endurance structures and **Gym** strength sessions
- **Exercises** — strength movements with prescriptions and optional video
- **Plans** — multi-week blueprints with share visibility (Private / Team / Public)

See [Coach Library & Sharing](/documentation/coaches/coach-library) and [Strength Training for Coaches](/documentation/coaches/strength-programming).

### 7. Optional: team and groups

- **Coaching → Team** — professional team, staff roles, invite code
- **Athlete groups** — organize rosters without changing relationships

See [Team Management](/documentation/coaches/team-management) and [Athlete Groups](/documentation/coaches/athlete-groups).

### 8. Analytics and messaging expectations

- **Coaching → Analytics** — personal, single-athlete, and multi-athlete charts ([Coach Analytics](/documentation/coaches/coach-analytics))
- **Message Athlete** — opens **AI Chat** with a starter prompt about that athlete; it does **not** send email or an in-app DM to the athlete. Use your normal channel (or Telegram if the athlete uses it) for human messaging. See [Messaging & AI-Assisted Review](/documentation/coaches/messaging-ai-review).

## Permissions to remember

Access is relationship- and role-scoped. A team roster entry does not always grant private data. Athletes can revoke you. Read [Permissions & Privacy](/documentation/coaches/permissions-privacy) before inviting staff or publishing intake forms.

## Checklist after week one

- [ ] At least one connected athlete
- [ ] Public presence / Start page configured if you want inbound leads
- [ ] A few library workouts you can reuse
- [ ] Comfortable scheduling and reviewing on **Coaching → Calendar**
- [ ] Clear on what Message Athlete does (and does not do)
