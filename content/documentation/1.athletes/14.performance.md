The **Performance** page shows how your fitness evolves over time. It combines training stress metrics with personal records and trend analysis.

## Training stress chart

The core chart displays three curves:

| Metric            | Window                 | Meaning                                |
| ----------------- | ---------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| **CTL (Fitness)** | 42-day rolling average | Long-term training capacity            |
| **ATL (Fatigue)** | 7-day rolling average  | Short-term training stress             |
| **TSB (Form)**    | CTL − ATL              | Readiness — positive often means fresh |

Use TSB to gauge race readiness: deeply negative TSB suggests accumulated fatigue; positive TSB suggests you are fresh (but possibly detrained if sustained too long).

See [Metrics & Scoring](/documentation/athletes/metrics-scoring) for calculation details.

## Personal bests

**Performance → Bests** tracks records across distances and durations:

- Peak power outputs (5s, 1min, 5min, 20min, 60min)
- Best pace at standard distances
- Seasonal and all-time records

Bests update automatically as new workouts sync.

## Fitness page

**Fitness** provides deeper analysis of your aerobic and anaerobic development:

- Power-duration curve
- Efficiency factor trends
- VO2 max estimates from connected devices

![Current Fitness analysis card with an overall score, summary, adaptation analysis, and a recommended action](/media/docs/fitness-trends.jpg)

Open the score card when you want the context behind a change in fitness rather than relying on the trend line alone.

## Analytics tools

Advanced analysis lives under **Library → Charts** and **Analytics**:

- **Workout Comparison** — compare two sessions side by side
- **Workout Explorer** — search and filter your history by type, zone, or metric
- **Report Builder** — custom charts for specific metrics over time

## Using trends in coaching

Combine performance data with Chat for deeper insight:

- _"Is my CTL trending up too fast?"_
- _"Compare my last two threshold sessions."_
- _"Am I on track for my goal event based on current fitness?"_

## Troubleshooting load charts

### Form (TSB) looks “wrong”

Form is **CTL − ATL**. A hard training block drives Form negative; rest raises it. If numbers disagree with feel:

1. Confirm recent workouts synced and duplicates are resolved ([Duplicate Workouts](/documentation/athletes/duplicate-workouts)).
2. Check sport thresholds used for TSS ([Sport Profiles](/documentation/athletes/settings/sport-profiles)).
3. Remember CTL (~42 days) moves slowly; ATL (~7 days) moves quickly after a big weekend.

### Fitness dropped after a rest week

Expected when ATL falls faster than CTL at first, then CTL eases if rest continues. Compare against your plan taper rather than day-to-day noise.

### Bests missing a known PR

Open the source workout, confirm it is the **primary** version, and that power or pace streams imported. Re-sync the provider or re-analyze if streams were incomplete.
